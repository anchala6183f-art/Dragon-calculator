How to Use

Getting Started

Dragon Calculator works just like the native calculator applications you are already familiar with on modern smartphones. It is designed to offer zero learning curve, instant page loads, and absolutely no complex nested menus. To start a calculation, simply load the page, click or tap the numerical buttons, and execute your equations.

Core Arithmetic Operations

Our calculator supports standard algebraic sequence logic. To perform an operation, input your first number, choose an operator button, input your second number, and press the equals (=) button to view the result.

1. Addition (+)

Used to combine two or more numerical values together. To execute this, enter the initial value, tap the + button, enter the subsequent value, and press the = button.

Example: To calculate 154 + 289, press: 1 5 4 + 2 8 9 =. The main display will immediately show 443.

2. Subtraction (−)

Used to find the difference between two numbers. Enter the primary value, tap the subtraction operator, enter the value to be subtracted, and tap equals.

Example: To calculate 500 − 175, press: 5 0 0 − 1 7 5 =. The display will read 325.

3. Multiplication (×)

Used to scale one value by another. Input your first value, tap the multiplication operator, enter the multiplier, and press the equals key.

Example: To calculate 12 × 15, press: 1 2 × 1 5 =. The main display will show the product 180.

4. Division (÷)

Used to split a number into equal portions. Enter the dividend value, tap the division operator, enter the divisor, and tap the equals button.

Example: To divide 450 by 6, press: 4 5 0 ÷ 6 =. The calculator will output 75.

Advanced Functions and Modifiers

Decimal Point (.) for Precision

For working with fractional or non-whole numbers, use the decimal point button. You can enter decimals for both the first and second values of your calculations.

Example: To multiply 4.5 by 3.2, press: 4 . 5 × 3 . 2 =. The display will read 14.4.

Percentage calculations (%)

The percentage function works as a quick decimal scale converter (dividing the current value by 100), making it extremely useful for compounding financial equations, calculating customer discounts, or estimating tips.

Example: To find 25% of 120, first input the base value: 1 2 0, press the multiplication key: ×, input the percentage portion: 2 5, press the percentage key: % (which turns 25 into 0.25 on display), and finally tap = to output 30.

Sign Toggler Key (+/−)

Toggles the mathematical sign of the currently displayed number between positive and negative. This is perfect for accounting calculations or working with negative temperature scales.

Example: To input -15 + 40, press: 1 5 +/− (the display will now show -15), then tap + 4 0 =. The display will read 25.

All Clear Button (AC)

The AC key completely resets the layout. Clicking it will empty the numerical register, clear any pending operations, and reset the main output window back to 0.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

1. What to do if you see an "Error" or "NaN" Message?

An "Error" or "NaN" (Not a Number) indication typically occurs when performing mathematically invalid operations. The most frequent cause is attempting to divide any number by zero (e.g., 8 ÷ 0 = Error). To resolve this, simply tap the AC button on your keypad to reset the memory registers, and begin your entry sequence again with valid integers.

2. Large Numbers and Visual Layout Scaling

If you perform massive calculations that result in many digits, the calculator's responsive layout is programmed to automatically shrink the font size of the main display screen. This ensures you can read your complete results without text overflowing or getting truncated along the edges. If your calculations exceed 15 digits, we recommend rounding your figures or clearing the screen via AC to avoid display limitations.

3. Physical Keyboard Support

If you are accessing this application on a desktop computer, Chromebook, or with a physical Bluetooth keyboard connected to your tablet, you can use your computer's standard number pad. The number keys, arithmetic operators (+, -, *, /), Enter key (for equals), and Backspace/Escape keys are fully mapped for high-productivity input.
